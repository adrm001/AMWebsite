Introduction

GaeaGun is a turn based tactical multiplayer game. Terra-form the playing field to your advantage and defeat your opponent and conquer the Island, or be conquered by the fiery lava.

 

Gameplay

The object of the game is to destroy your all of your enemy's tanks and claim the island yours. Shoot the tanks down or sink them into the burning lava, both methods are just as effective. At the beginning of your turn, you can move all of your tanks, be careful not to make wasteful moves though, as you only have a certain amount of "Move Points" per turn. Once you have situated your tanks strategically, just aim and shoot. The projection trajectory tells you where your shot would land if you were to shoot at exactly 50% power.

Technical Details

The major tecnhical accomplishment of this game is real-time terrain deformation. The terrain is stored as a regular 3-Dimensional grid of points, each of which stores the signed distance to the surface, positive distance for inside the surface, and negative distace for outside the surface. To display the terrain as a mesh, an algorithim known as the Marching Cubes Algorithm is used. This algorithm looks at each of the cubes (called voxels) that constitute the grid. Each voxel has 8 vertices, each of which has the distace to the surface. If a sign change is detected along any of the edges, then it is known that the surface intersects the Voxel along that edge. There are 256 possiblities of how a surface sould possibly intersect the voxel, each of which is enumerated on a table. The computaion is then reduced to a lookup on a big table, indexed by which of the edges are being intersected. Then along each intersected edge, linear interpolation is used to find out exactly where on the edge the surface crosses (for more details on Marchign Cubes refer to the links on the References section of this page).

To make displaying and updating the terrain faster, the field is cut up into many smaller sections, each of which stores a vertex array representing the triangles that were generated by Marching Cubes. When an explosion creates a creater, a sphere is cut out of the appropiate part of the terrain. To do this, all of the points in the 3D grid are updated with new distances. Marching Cubes is then re-run only on the affected areas to update the mesh. This proccess is very fast and takes less than .5 secs to complete

A simple physics engine and particle system governs the interactions of the objects in the game. Collision detection with the terrain is done in near constant time by using tri-linear interpolation of the values in the 3D grid of points (this gives the distance to the terrain ant any point within the defined space). To calculate the normals (used for navigation of the tanks) the normal value at each point in the grid is calculated by computing the gradient vector (the gradient at a particular position for a scalar field is equal to the normal of the isosurface at that iso value). Tri-linear interpolation is then used to calculate the normal at any postion.

Perlin noise was also used to randomly generate the terrain, and also to create the texture for the explosion and the lava. To animate the lava a simple shader that takes the sine of the intensity value plus time was used.
