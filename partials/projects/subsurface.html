Subsurface scattering

A technique for rendering translucent materials.

Click here to download the paper

For my undergrad thesis project I investigated techniques used for rendering translucent materials. For typical opaque materials, the rendering problem can be localized. This means that most of the effects that contribute to the materials final reflected color can be computed by sampling the direction and amount of light at a particular point. This information is usually represented by a bidirectional reflectance distribution function, or BRDF.

However, translucent materials cannot be modeled like this. This is because in translucent materials, light does not simply bounce off the surface. Instead, the light actually penetrates the material, bounces around a few times and comes out at a different spot. This makes the resulting reflected color at a particular point a function of the incoming light in an area around that point. This can be modeled with a bidirectional surface scattering reflectance distribution function, or BSSRDF.

This property is found in skin and marble, for example. My undergraduate thesis work combines two techniques in order to speed up computing the interaction between different layers of the material.
